About Lake Chad

Lake Chad is a historically large, shallow, endorheic lake in Africa, which has varied in size over the centuries. It is an important source of fresh water and livelihood for millions of people in the surrounding areas, including Nigeria, Chad, Cameroon, and Niger.

Unfortunately, the lake has been shrinking rapidly in recent decades due to climate change and human activities. The reduction in the lake's area has had a profound impact on the surrounding communities who depend on it for their livelihood. From fishing to farming and livestock rearing, Lake Chad plays a crucial role in the socio-economic activities of these communities.

Our mission is to conserve and restore Lake Chad, reversing its reduction and ensuring it continues to provide vital resources for local communities and wildlife. We invite you to learn more about our efforts, the lake's history, and how you can help. Together, we can make a difference and preserve Lake Chad for future generations.
